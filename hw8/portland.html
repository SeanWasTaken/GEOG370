Does Portland's bike infrastructure support all residents or just wealthier ones?

Question and Why I chose it

Something that I'm interested in is moving away from car dependent infrastructure, and a major part of this is building new bike infrastructure meant for getting from place to place and not just recreation. I saw that Portland has made large improvements to its bike infrastructure, but wanted to examine if these new investments have benefitted everyone, or just more affluent neighborhoods.

Methods Used

From Portland's GIS website I found a CSV of all bike parking and shapefiles for reccomended bike routes, the area covered by the city's bike share program, and the economic vulnerability of residents of different neighborhoods. First I made a 100 meter buffer around all the recommended bike routes and bike parking spots. Next I did the symmetric difference between this buffer and the area covered by the economic vulnerability shapefile to create a polygon with the areas covered by bike infrastructure not included. I dissolved this polygon to make it easier to understand and placed it over the economic vulnerability chloropleth, creating a map of the economic vulnerabilities supported by Portland's bike infrastructure.
